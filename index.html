EXPERIENCE

I was a teaching assistant for Quantitative Biological Methods (QBM) Molecular Biology I, and Medical Biochemistry. I taught lab lessons, wrote exam questions/put exams together, proctored exams, ran office hours, and edited the QBM course textbook.

During graduate school, I have experienced a variety of different lab settings. My laboratory rotations included a biochemistry lab focused on protein structure elucidation (crystallography), where I plated carbonic anhydrase protein crystals and a molecular cell biology lab, where I learned how to culture Plasmodium falciparum asexually and facilitate gametocyte exflaggelation.

Since joining Neuroscience, I have learned many different techniques, including murine brain microdissection, primary culture (cortex, midbrain), live-cell calcium imaging, AAV construct design, confocal microscopy, ICC/IF, IHC, and western blotting. In addition, I have learned and used Python programming to analyze live-cell imaging and electrophysiology data, involving both single-cell and functional neuronal connectivity metrics.
I've presented at local and national conferences. Sadly, (thanks, COVID!) no international conferences yet. I won 3rd places amongst graduate students at the NCF SfN poster competition in 2019. I wrote a review, which was published earlier this year, and am finalizing my dissertation research at the moment.

In August 2021, I participated in the Neuromatch Academy: Deep Learning course, where I learned about various types of neural networks (convolutional neural networks [CNNs], recurrent neural networks [RNNs], generative adversarial networks [GANs], etc.) and worked on a group project optimizing feature extraction for training a neural net.
